Chapter 5 Hands-On Practice Case

1.

  • The JavaJam website uses a hierarchical design.
  • For the content delivered by the JavaJam site, the hierarchical design is the most appropriate.
  • There's no reason for the site's content pages to follow a linear layout as the information on those pages are not reliant on having seen a previous page's information.
  • A random layout would make finding the information a visitor is interested in needlessly difficult to find.

2.

  • Design practices the site does well:

    1. The site uses an appropriate color scheme, including good text to background contrast.
    2. Consistent layout and design of each page on the site, including navigation and content areas.
    3. Information on the site is easy to find and clearly organized.

  • Design practices that could be improved:

    1. By following the JavaJam case studies, not all the navigation hyperlinks work. The job page does not exist. (I created a temporary page for the Jobs link for my own sanity.)
    2. No navigational aids are used on the page. There are no links in the footer for accessiblity nor a skip to content link.
    3. There is no measure of timeliness on the page. The music page has acts for January and Febuary, but no year, and those months have come and gone.

  • Other possible improvements:

    1. The navigation links are somewhat bland and could also be moved to the left of the screen.
    2. Either the Jobs page should be created or the link removed until it is ready, so the user doesn't get a "Page not found" error.
    3. The menu could using something more interesting than just text descriptions of products.
